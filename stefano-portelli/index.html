I am a researcher in urban anthropology, currently a postdoctoral fellow at the University of Roma Tre; I am a member of the Catalan Institute of Anthropology, of University of Barcelona's OACU (Observatory of Anthropology of Urban Conflict) and GRECS (Grup de Recerca en Exclusió i Control Social). I am a former affiliate to Leicester University's Department of Geography and to Harvard University's Department of Anthropology. I conducted ethnographic research in Rome, Barcelona, Casablanca, Managua, and consultancy activities for NGOs and international public agencies. I organized Harvard's 1st Urban activism conference, and was an invited speaker at the 2023 Boston University Initiative on Cities Gentrification and Displacement conference, and a keynote speaker at 2021 Catalan Conference in Anthropology (CoCA) My mentors are Professors Loretta Lees, Michael Herzfeld, Manuel Delgado. I also grew up with an oral historian.

I work on the social and cultural impact of development induced displacement and gentrification, and campaign for the right to housing for all. I defend squatting and self-built neighborhoods as guarantees of the social function of private property, and consider rent control as the only policy that may prevent gentrification and displacement. I am interested in rituals and religions of the working-classes as devices that help to maintain relations inside communities, to sustain bonds with the environment, and to face the state, in times of crises.

In 2017-2020 I was granted a EU-funded Marie Curie Global Fellowship, and in 2022 I reached the 2nd step of evaluation for an ERC Starting Grant project. I am especially fond of the Moroccan Gnawa cultural complex, and I support Casablanca's Gnaoua Culture association. I am the founder of Barcelona's Periferies Urbanes research group and website; I always write on the Italian website Napoli Monitor, and on the independent journal Lo stato delle città.

I campaign for the right to move and for the right to stay put; I support self-determination and liberation from exploitation, colonization and neocolonization for all people on earth, including the Kurdish and the Palestinian people, undergoing decades of violent occupation, apartheid and violation of basic rights, in breach of international treaties and UN resolutions, for the sake of corporate profit and political supremacy. Israel's genocide of the Palestinian people exposed the misery of an international order based on white suprematism and hypocrisy, which is our duty to dismantle, from the bottom up, with all means necessary.
